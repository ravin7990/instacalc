What is a CAGR Calculation Calculator?

A compound annual growth rate calculator computes the annualized return of an investment over a certain period. Unlike simple returns, CAGR includes the impact of compounding, giving a clearer picture of performance.

  • ✔️ What is my average annual growth rate?
  • ✔️ How has my portfolio performed over time?
  • ✔️ How does this investment compare with others?

How the Online CAGR Calculator Works

The formula used by our online CAGR calculator is:

CAGR = (EV / BV)(1/t) - 1

Where:

  • EV – Ending Value
  • BV – Beginning Value
  • t – Number of years

How to Use This CAGR Annual Growth Rate Calculator

To use our CAGR annual growth rate calculator, just follow these simple steps:

  1. 1️⃣ Enter your Initial Investment (BV)
  2. 2️⃣ Enter your Final Investment Value (EV)
  3. 3️⃣ Input the Duration (Years)
  4. 4️⃣ Click on ‘Calculate’

Example of CAGR Calculation

Imagine you invested ₹1,00,000 and it grew to ₹1,80,000 over 5 years. Here's the math:

CAGR = (1,80,000 / 1,00,000)(1/5) - 1 = 12.57%

Limitations of CAGR

CAGR has some limitations:

  • 🔹 It assumes steady growth
  • 🔹 It does not show volatility or risk
  • 🔹 Past CAGR does not guarantee future results

FAQs

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is a measure of the annual growth rate of an investment over a specified period, assuming the profits are reinvested at the end of each year. It provides a smoothed annual rate of return.
The CAGR Calculator calculates the annual growth rate of an investment using the formula:
CAGR = (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1 / Number of Years) - 1
You need to input the beginning value, ending value, and the number of years to calculate the CAGR.
You need to provide:
  • Beginning Value: The initial value of the investment.
  • Ending Value: The final value of the investment.
  • Number of Years: The duration of the investment in years.
