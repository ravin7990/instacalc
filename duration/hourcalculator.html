Professional Time Tracking

Calculating the gap between two times isn't just about subtraction. When crossing midnights or accounting for lunch breaks, precise tools are essential. Our calculator handles complex spans and converts them into the formats you need most.

For Payroll & Timesheets

Most payroll systems require time in decimal format (e.g., 7.5 hours instead of 7 hours 30 mins). This tool automatically performs that conversion so you don't have to.

Does it handle overnight shifts?

Yes. If your shift starts at 10:00 PM and ends at 6:00 AM the next day, the calculator uses the Date metadata to correctly identify the 8-hour span.

How is "Decimal Hours" calculated?

We divide the number of minutes by 60 and add it to the hours. 15 mins becomes 0.25, 30 mins becomes 0.50, and 45 mins becomes 0.75.
