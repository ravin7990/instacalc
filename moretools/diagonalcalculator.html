How to Calculate Diagonal of Rectangle

The formula for calculating the diagonal of a rectangle is:

Diagonal (d) = √(width² + height²)

Where:

  • Width = Horizontal side of the rectangle
  • Height = Vertical side of the rectangle
  • Diagonal = The line connecting opposite corners

This is also known as the diagonal of rectangle formula, and it's based on the Pythagorean Theorem.

Example Calculation

Let’s say your rectangle is 12 cm wide and 5 cm tall:

Diagonal = √(12² + 5²) = √(144 + 25) = √169 = 13 cm

So, the diagonal length is 13 cm.

Using Diagonal Formula for Square

If you have a square, use this diagonal of square formula:

Diagonal = side × √2

For example, if each side is 10 cm: Diagonal = 10 × √2 ≈ 14.14 cm
