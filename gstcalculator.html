Understanding GST Calculations

Our online tools make tax calculations straightforward for both inclusive and exclusive scenarios:


Add GST (Price + GST):
Formula: GST Amount = (Original Price × GST Percentage) / 100
Example: If price is 1,000 and GST is 18%, tax is 180, total is 1,180.


Remove GST (Price inclusive of GST):
Formula: GST Amount = Price - (Price × (100 / (100 + GST Percentage)))
Example: If total bill is 1,180 and tax is 18%, tax is 180, original net price is 1,000.


Enable the "Advanced Mode" to handle quantity calculations and see the exact split between Central (CGST), State (SGST), or Integrated (IGST) taxes.

What exactly is CGST, SGST, and IGST?

CGST stands for Central GST and SGST for State GST. These are applied on sales within the same state (Intra-state). IGST (Integrated GST) is applied when selling to a different state (Inter-state).

How many GST slabs are currently in India?

India currently uses four major tax slabs: 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Some items are exempt (0%). Our calculator supports all standard slabs.

Why use a GST calculator instead of manual math?

A calculator eliminates rounding errors and provides an instant breakdown of tax types, ensuring your billing or tax filing is accurate every single time.
