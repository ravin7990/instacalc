How is EPF maturity calculated in India?

EPF maturity is calculated by adding your monthly 12% employee contribution, the 3.67% employer share, and optionally your VPF contribution, then compounding the final balance annually using the latest EPFO declared interest rate (e.g., 8.25%).

What is the difference between EPF and EPS?

EPF (Employee Provident Fund) builds your compounding lump-sum retirement corpus. EPS (Employee Pension Scheme) takes 8.33% of the employer's share (capped at ₹1,250/month) to fund a fixed monthly pension paid to you after your retirement.

Can I include VPF in EPF calculations?

Yes. Voluntary Provident Fund (VPF) allows you to voluntarily contribute more than the mandated 12% of your basic salary. Our advanced calculator lets you add a VPF percentage to accurately project a significantly higher tax-free retirement corpus.

How does a salary hike impact my EPF corpus?

Since EPF is a strict percentage of your Basic Salary + DA, an annual salary hike mechanically increases your absolute monthly contribution. This continuous step-up effect radically boosts your final corpus via long-term geometric compounding.

Can I model partial EPF withdrawal before retirement?

Yes. EPF rules allow partial tax-free withdrawals for specific life events (marriage, home purchase, medical emergencies). You can input a one-time withdrawal year and amount to instantly see how early liquidity severely impacts your final retirement balance.

Does this EPF calculator show inflation-adjusted corpus?

Yes. Viewing a massive nominal corpus value can be misleading. By setting an expected inflation rate, our tool mathematically discounts your final EPF maturity amount to show its exact true purchasing power in today's currency.

What is the maximum EPF interest rate?

The EPF interest rate is not fixed; it is actively declared by the Central Board of Trustees (EPFO) every financial year. In recent years, it has fluctuated between 8.1% and 8.5%, making it one of the highest-yielding sovereign-backed debt instruments in India.

Is my EPF corpus completely tax-free?

Generally, yes. EPF falls under the EEE (Exempt-Exempt-Exempt) tax regime. However, if your total employee contribution (EPF + VPF) exceeds ₹2.5 lakh in a single financial year, the interest actively earned on the excess amount becomes fully taxable according to your slab.

How is the EPS pension amount estimated?

EPS pension is broadly estimated using the formula: (Pensionable Salary × Pensionable Service) ÷ 70. Our calculator utilizes this government statutory logic to project an indicative figure of your guaranteed monthly pension at retirement.

Can I opt out of the EPS pension contribution?

No. If your Basic Pay at the time of joining was under ₹15,000, EPS deduction is strictly mandatory. If your joining Basic Pay was above ₹15,000, you and your employer can jointly opt to push the entire 12% into the EPF corpus without EPS allocation.

Why do people use the Wage Cap mode for EPF?

The EPFO statutory wage cap is ₹15,000/month. The 'Wage Cap Mode' restricts the employer's 12% matching contribution specifically to this ₹15,000 limit (₹1,800 total). Switching this toggle lets you accurately see how statutory limits throttle corpus growth.

What is EPF Target Planning?

If you have a fixed retirement corpus goal (e.g., ₹5 Crores), our advanced EPF Target Planner uses a mathematical binary search to exactly calculate the additional VPF percentage you must proactively deduct from your salary today to successfully close the gap.

Can I transfer my EPF account when I change jobs?

Yes, your Universal Account Number (UAN) remains permanent throughout your career. When switching employers, you seamlessly transfer your accumulated EPF balance online, ensuring uninterrupted compounding.

What happens if I stop contributing to EPF?

If you quit your job or start a business, your existing EPF corpus continues to actively earn compound interest for up to 3 years. After 3 years of zero fresh contributions, the account becomes inoperative and ceases to accumulate interest.
